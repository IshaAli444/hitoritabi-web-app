A. Mori

Tokyo, Japan Poet & Translator Contributor since 2023

A. Mori writes about the spaces between languages, the quiet of train stations at dawn, and the fragments of conversations overheard but never completed. Their work examines translation as both an act of betrayal and an act of love.

About the Author

Born in Kyoto, raised between Tokyo and Vancouver, A. Mori's writing exists in the liminal space between cultures. They describe their process as "listening to the silence between words" — capturing what remains unsaid in conversations, letters, and city sounds.

When not writing, Mori works as a literary translator, bringing contemporary Japanese poetry into English. They believe translation is not about finding equivalents, but about discovering new rooms in the house of language.
